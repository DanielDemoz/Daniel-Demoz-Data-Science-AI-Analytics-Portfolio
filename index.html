Bio

How I work with organizations

Most of my career sits across three places: the classroom, client organizations, and my independent practice, BRUKD Consultancy. That mix keeps a useful tension: academic rigor on one side, and delivery constraints on the other.

Teaching graduate analytics keeps me close to tools, assignments, and the gap between theory and how work actually gets done. Client work keeps me close to budgets, vendors, and leaders who need plain language. The thread I protect is the handoff from analysis to action: KPIs people believe, data quality you can defend, and pilots small enough to learn from without betting the brand.

When teams talk about AI, I start with readiness and ownership. Models are easy compared with getting agreement on what “good” looks like and who is accountable when the output is wrong.

SMBs and institutions differ in scale, not in the questions that matter: who signs off, what you will measure in the first ninety days, and what you stop if drift or misuse shows up. Evidence is the stack of baselines and review points that let you defend a decision months later.

Strong data and AI work should sharpen judgment, not replace it.
